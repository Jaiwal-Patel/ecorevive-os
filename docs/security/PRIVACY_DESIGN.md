# Privacy Design

## Data minimization

Collect only information needed to coordinate pickups, verify recycler handovers and communicate impact. Do not collect identity documents, payment details or unrelated household information.

## Access principles

- residents see their own requests;
- volunteers see assigned requests only;
- corporate coordinators see authorized organizational requests;
- operations administrators see information needed to coordinate collections;
- public visitors see aggregated impact only;
- governance status does not automatically justify routine access to all household data.

## Public reporting

Never publish exact household coordinates, addresses, telephone numbers or low-count neighborhood statistics that could identify a participant.

## Retention

Before launch, executives must approve a retention schedule for contact data, request records, photos, receipts, audit events and backups. Deletion requests must account for legitimate operational and legal recordkeeping needs.
